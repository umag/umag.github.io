3ml of Crop 2017

I really love Crop 2017 by The House of Oud. I had a 3ml sample of it, now it’s empty.

It smells like forest after the rain — moss, oud, autumn. More precisely it smells like the air outside an onsen in Hakone, up the mountain in the woods, after it rained. Not the water, not the steam. The air you’re breathing while you sit in the hot water. That’s what it gives me back.

You can’t buy it. 547 numbered bottles, 2017, 100% natural, gone.

So yesterday I went for a walk, ended up in a perfume shop, and asked for alternatives. I described it — forest after rain, moss, autumn, anise. Then I pulled up the Fragrantica notes and showed them the actual list: agarwood, black licorice, star anise, resins, sandalwood. The full spec, handed over. They were helpful and gave me many things, all of them forest-green-fresh.

Which is exactly wrong. Crop 2017 is soft spicy 100, anis 80, oud 76, then balsamic, amber, powdery. It’s dry and dark and resinous. They heard “forest after rain” and matched the words — green, pine, fresh.

So I came home and built a swamp extension for Fragrantica to run the query properly.

Fragrantica does not want to be queried. Its own /search/ page is a decoy: it returns perfume ids 1, 3, 4, 5, 6 regardless of what you ask for, because real search is client-side Algolia. The Algolia key is sitting right there in window.fragranticaRuntime, and it answers Invalid API key from every angle, including with the referer spoofed. And a real headless Chrome loading /search/ gets a Cloudflare Turnstile widget where the search box should be. Two independent locks. So search goes out through DuckDuckGo’s HTML endpoint and comes back in via the perfume URLs, which are public and indexed anyway. (robots.txt allows /perfume/, /designers/, /notes/ — and disallows ClaudeBot outright, which I enjoyed.)

Then the actual question. I added a find-by-notes method to intersect note pages — black licorice ∩ agarwood, the exact signature. 38 candidates, and every one of them noise: Aventus, Bleu de Chanel, 4711, Lattafa Khamrah. The intersection is useless because Fragrantica’s notes are boolean. A trace of amberwood counts the same as an oud spine.

The same mistake the shop made. Both of them matched tokens.

The “similar” list doesn’t help either. The heading is literally “People who like this also like” — it’s purchase correlation. For Crop 2017 it returns the expensive-niche shelf: Amouage, Malle, Tom Ford, Serge Lutens. Same price tier, same taste tribe, nothing about how it smells.

And the internet fails in the same direction. Community consensus says Crop 2016 is “very similar”, so I pulled both through the model: 2016 is green 100, oud 77, mint and tea. It shares the oud and the sandalwood base with 2017 and nothing else. Everyone converges on green. Green is the one thing it isn’t.

Final answer: there’s no copy. Clone houses only dupe bestsellers, and nobody clones a 547-piece natural. The anise-licorice-over-real-oud is the whole point of the thing, which is exactly why it can’t be bought.

A bit of disappointment. And then the thought I actually walked away with: maybe I need to assemble it myself.

The shop had my full note list and got it wrong. The database had the same list and returned 4711. So the notes were never the smell — they’re words about it, and what I want is the air on that mountain. If the map can’t hold it and no vendor sells it, there’s only genesis left.

@magistr/fragrantica@2026.07.15.1, 6 methods, Grade A. It works. It just told me the truth.
